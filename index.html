NEYMAR: The Last Joga Bonito

Almost everyone who has watched football in the last decade has marveled at Neymar's dexterity on the field. Even on the biggest stage, dominated by two of the greatest football 'GOATs,' he shone brightly. Playing with flair and finesse, his style made it impossible not to admire the beauty of his game.

Neymar's Early Days

Neymar's footballing career started in his home country, where he showed himself as one player to watch out for. He immediately caught the attention of the biggest clubs in world football. He is recognized as the only player to make it into the Top 30 Ballon d'Or shortlist while playing outside the top five leagues.

Neymar's Prime

Neymar's prime was a dazzling display of skill, flair, and creativity, making him one of the most electrifying footballers of his generation. During his peak years at Barcelona (2014-2017) and Paris Saint-Germain (2017-2020), he combined blistering pace, exceptional dribbling, and clinical finishing to dominate defenses. As part of the legendary MSN trio with Messi and Suárez, he played a key role in Barcelona's 2015 treble-winning season, scoring in the Champions League final. At PSG, he became the focal point, leading them to a Champions League final in 2020. His prime was defined by mesmerizing footwork, audacious tricks, and an ability to decide games single-handedly, cementing his legacy as one of football’s greatest entertainers.

The Prince of Football

The end of Neymar's career was marked by a mix of brilliance, injuries, and unfulfilled potential. While still showcasing glimpses of his genius, persistent injuries began to take a toll, limiting his impact on the pitch. His time at Paris Saint-Germain ended with growing frustrations, as he struggled to lead them to Champions League glory despite flashes of individual brilliance. A high-profile move to Al Hilal in Saudi Arabia signaled the winding down of his time at the top levels of European football, shifting the focus from competition to commercial appeal. Though still a global icon, the latter years of his career lacked the same explosiveness.
